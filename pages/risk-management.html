Risk Management Guide

Risk management is the most important aspect of successful forex trading. Even the best strategy will fail without proper risk management. This guide will teach you how to protect your capital and trade like a professional.

The Golden Rule: Never Risk More Than You Can Afford to Lose

Before you start trading, decide how much money you're willing to risk. This should be capital that won't affect your daily life if lost. Never trade with borrowed money or funds needed for essential expenses.

Key Risk Management Strategies

1. Use Stop Loss Orders

A stop loss is your safety net. It automatically closes your trade if the market moves against you by a predetermined amount. Never enter a trade without setting a stop loss.

üí° Pro Tip: Set your stop loss based on technical analysis, not arbitrary numbers. Look for support/resistance levels or use the ATR indicator.

2. The 1% Rule

Risk only 1% of your trading capital on each trade. If you have a $1,000 account, risk only $10 per trade. This ensures that even a series of losses won't devastate your account.

3. Risk-Reward Ratio

Always aim for a favorable risk-reward ratio. A minimum of 1:2 is recommended, meaning for every dollar you risk, you should aim to make at least two dollars. This way, you can be profitable even with a 50% win rate.

Diversification

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Avoid trading too many correlated pairs simultaneously. For example, EUR/USD and GBP/USD often move together, so opening positions on both increases your risk.

Emotional Control

Emotions are the enemy of good risk management:

  • Fear - Don't close winning trades too early
  • Greed - Don't increase position sizes after wins
  • Revenge Trading - Never try to recover losses quickly
  • Overconfidence - Stick to your risk rules even during winning streaks

Keep a Trading Journal

Document every trade including:

  1. Entry and exit points
  2. Position size
  3. Reason for the trade
  4. Emotions felt during the trade
  5. Lessons learned

Account Management Rules

Professional Traders Follow These Rules:
  • Maximum daily loss limit: 2-3% of account
  • Maximum weekly loss limit: 5% of account
  • Stop trading after 3 consecutive losses
  • Withdraw profits regularly
  • Never increase risk to recover losses

Leverage Management

While brokers may offer high leverage (1:500 or more), using it is dangerous. Professional traders typically use leverage of 1:10 to 1:50. Lower leverage = lower risk.
